System Dynamics & Strategic Planning

Wildlife Management & Economic Simulation

Tool: Vensim
Archetype: Limits to Growth
Context: Wildlife management - hunting club

About this project:
This project utilizes System Dynamics to model the complex management of a hunting association. It simulates the delicate balance between wildlife population control and the economic impact of crop damages. The model specifically investigates how information delays and financial pressures (repaying damages to farmers) can lead to system instability.

Tools & Methods
Vensim System Dynamics Simulation Modeling Causal Loop Diagrams (CLD) Stock & Flow

1. The Problem: Managing Uncertainty

The core challenge is planning the hunting quota to prevent overpopulation while managing finite financial resources. The model addresses critical real-world dynamics:

  • Crop Damages: Simulates the financial burden of repaying farmers for damages caused by game. It estimates the varying extent to which farmers actually claim these damages.
  • Information Delays: Hunters often plan quotas based on outdated or inaccurate census data. The model demonstrates how this delay causes the system to overshoot its limits.

2. Modeling Methodology (Vensim)

I constructed a Stock & Flow structure in Vensim to explicitly model the feedback loops driving the system's behavior:

  • Biological Growth (R): Modeling natural reproduction and population dynamics.
  • Financial Constraints (B): Balancing investments in prevention against the cost of damages.
  • Farmer Behavior: A behavioral component estimating the probability of farmers enforcing damage claims.

3. Interactive Dashboard

A key deliverable was a user-friendly interface designed for both game managers and laypeople. It allows users to experiment with constants and observe long-term consequences:

  • Control Panel: Users can adjust variables like Initial Budget, Feed Costs, or Desired Population Size.
  • Real-time Simulation: Immediate visualization of how decisions impact Cash Flow and Population Stability over a 100-year timeline.

4. Scenarios Analyzed & Findings

The simulation revealed that without accounting for information delays, the system naturally tends towards oscillation or financial collapse.

Scenario A: Target Population Impact

Comparing trends when planning for different population targets. Targeting a population size above the forest's carrying capacity proves unsustainable, causing significantly larger fluctuations in the financial function over time compared to a conservative target.

Scenario B: Measurement Accuracy

Analyzing how census accuracy affects stability. Inaccurate population counting leads to severe oscillations in both cash flow and wildlife stock. The population frequently overshoots the maximum capacity or crashes, unlike scenarios with precise measurement.

Personal Reflection

This project required a shift from linear thinking to Systems Thinking. By modeling the Limits to Growth archetype, I learned how to identify hidden constraints in a system and how time delays can turn a stable growth curve into a destructive cycle. It highlighted that sustainable management isn't about maximizing growth, but about managing limits.
