The Challenge: Farming isn't just about feeding animals; it's a complex system of delayed feedbacks, biological constraints, and volatile market prices. This project translates real-world agricultural logic into a Business Intelligence & Simulation tool.

The core philosophy was to bridge the gap between Reality and Data Science. It combines System Modeling with Financial Planning to create a digital twin of the farm, allowing for risk-free testing of strategies before investing capital.

1. System Modeling: From Reality to CLD

The project began in the field. I conducted in-depth interviews with a local sheep farmer to understand the "Physics" of the business. We discussed what is relevant to the model and what is unnecessarily complex.

Together, we mapped out the Causal Loop Diagram (CLD) to capture the system's structure, identifying critical feedback loops (e.g., Pasture Degradation) and delays (e.g., Gestation) that traditional Excel planning misses.

2. The Mathematical Engine (Python)

Based on the CLD, I built a robust Python simulation engine utilizing System Dynamics principles. Every variable in the model is stochastic with a normal distribution to simulate real-world variance. All data points and functions were fact-checked against academic sources and the farmer's experience.

Under the Hood: Variable Logic

Main State Variables (Stocks)
  • self.cash: income - total_out. Accumulates profit/loss over time.
  • self.hay_stock: harvest + buy - fed - sold. Physical inventory tracking.
  • self.ewes: keep - cull - deaths. Population dynamics of breeding females.
  • self.bcs: Body Condition Score. Driven by feed availability vs. lactation demand.
Decision Logic
  • wants_to_supplement: Triggered if perceived_bcs < 3.2.
  • cull_count: Removes sheep exceeding max age (Aging Chain).
  • pressure: demand_kg / available_grass. Determines pasture degradation.
  • market_logic: Tiered pricing (Local Premium limit vs Wholesale).
Economic Subsystem
  • income: Sum of Meat, Hay sales, and Subsidies.
  • total_out: Feed costs + Variable costs + Labor + Shocks.
  • day_admin: Diseconomy of scale: base * (animals/50)^factor.
  • labor_val: Calculation of labor costs based on hourly wages.
Environmental Feedback
  • weather_regime: Autocorrelated weather patterns (Dry/Wet trends).
  • pasture_health: Ecological loop. Degrades if pressure > 0.95.
  • shocks: Random probability events (Machinery breakdown, Disease).
  • seasonality: Winter/Summer cycles affecting growth and costs.

3. Streamlit BI Dashboard

The backend is powerful, but the frontend makes it usable. I developed a comprehensive Streamlit BI Dashboard that serves as a "Flight Simulator" for the farm. It goes beyond simple charts, offering a deep dive into the farm's operational and financial health.

Key features of the dashboard include:

  • Sticky KPI Header: Immediate feedback on Cash, Herd Size, Hay Stock, ROI, and Labor Hours.
  • Financial Overview: A stacked Cashflow analysis to pinpoint liquidity crunches and a cumulative profit tracker.
  • Herd Dynamics: An area chart visualizing the age structure and population growth over time.
  • Feeding Efficiency: A breakdown of feed sources (Grazing vs. Hay vs. Market Purchase) to optimize costs.
  • Biological Metrics: Tracking Body Condition Score (BCS) and Pasture Health to ensure sustainability.

4. Monte Carlo Laboratory

A single simulation isn't enough to manage risk. I implemented a Monte Carlo Laboratory that brings a true Data Science approach to the project.

This module runs the simulation thousands of times with randomized weather patterns and price volatilities. It generates probability distributions for outcomes like Bankruptcy Risk or Net Profit and includes Sensitivity Analysis to identify which variables (e.g., Fuel Price vs. Meat Price) have the highest correlation with success.
