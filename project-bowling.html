Bowling Team Analytics

A passion project for my hobby bowling team. We moved beyond simple scoreboards to settle friendly debates: Who chokes under pressure? And who is the most consistent player?

View Dashboard

1. The Challenge: Why we suck?

We noticed a paradox: We often felt like we played well (lots of pins down), but the final scores were average. We also had players who started strong but collapsed at the end.

  • The Efficiency Gap: Why don't 9 knocked pins translate to points?
  • The "Choke" Factor: Who performs better in the Late Game?
  • Form Tracking: Who is currently "hot" above their baseline?

2. Advanced DAX Engineering

I engineered custom measures using advanced table functions and dynamic filtering.

1. Weighted Form (Forma 2):
Calculates relative performance change using TOPN to compare the last played day against specific historical context.
-- Score of previous games (History Context) VAR skorePredchoziHry = SUMX( TOPN( pocetHerPosledniDen, hryPredchozi, 'Tabulka1'[Den], DESC, 'Tabulka1'[PořadíHry], DESC ), 'Tabulka1'[Skóre 10. kolo] ) -- Calculate % change vs History VAR vypocet = IF(NOT(ISBLANK(prumerPredchoziHry)), (prumerPosledniDen - prumerPredchoziHry) / prumerPredchoziHry, [Forma] ) RETURN vypocet
2. Relative Consistency (Virtual Table Aggregation):
Uses SUMMARIZE to build an in-memory table of players and their Coefficient of Variation (CoV), comparing individual stability against the team average.
-- Compare Player's consistency vs Team Average VAR prumer_CoV = CALCULATE( AVERAGEX( SUMMARIZE( Tabulka1, Tabulka1[Hráč], "CoV_Hráč", [koeficient variability mezi hody] ), [CoV_Hráč] ), ALL(Tabulka1) ) RETURN prumer_CoV - [koeficient variability mezi hody]

4. Key Findings

The data confirmed our suspicions about the "Efficiency Gap".

The "7-9 Pin" Trap

We consistently knocked down 7-9 pins but failed to score high because of poor spare conversion.

Clutch Performance

Identified players who thrive under pressure—either by converting critical strikes/spares or consistently dominating the second half of the game.

Personal Reflection

What started as fun for friends turned into a real technical challenge. Converting bowling rules—where the sequence of throws matters—into DAX formulas was harder than it looks. It taught me how to handle complex row context and logic better than any textbook example.
